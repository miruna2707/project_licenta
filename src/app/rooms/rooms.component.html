THE OLDEST AND HIGHEST RESORT IN ROMANIA

The Păltiniş mountain and tourist resort is located on the north-eastern slope of the Cindrel Mountains, 32 km away from the City of Sibiu. This is the oldest and highest mountain resort in Romania. The settlement located at an altitude of 1442 meters, below the Oncesti Peak, surrounded by coniferous trees, was set up by the Carpatina Transilvania Society at the end of the 19th century. Dating back to over a century ago, three of the over the 40 villas that had been built in time in this resort have been declared historic monuments, namely the Tourists ‘House, the Doctors’ House and the Monaco Hall. The main therapeutic factor is the alpine climate with strong odorizing air, rich in aerosols and ultraviolet radiation. The pure air quality in this resort is beneficial for treating asthenia, physical and intellectual burnout, anaemia, pulmonary sequelae, and developmental deficiencies in children.
